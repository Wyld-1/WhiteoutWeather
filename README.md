# White Weather

Accurate, fast, and beautiful, built directly around NOAA forecasts.

### What is this?

**NOAA weather** forecasts that just got **convient and beautiful**, that's what.

White Weather was born out of simple frustration. When we're checking for a weekend powder day and all we see is a generic snowflake icon, or when we have to open a web browser just to find 1990s graphics, we decided we needed better data and a better way to see it.

We took the gold standard of meteorological reporting — NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration) — and stripped away the clunky interface. The result is a high-performance, beautiful experience built for skiers, hikers, and anyone who wants the real deal from the experts.

---

### Why White Weather?

- **Accurate.** We display the exact forecasts found on NOAA's webpage, so you won't have to reach for your browser again.
- **Complete.** We don’t hide snow and rain totals. If it’s going to dump, you’ll see the estimated accumulation for every day of the week right on the main 7-day forecast.
- **Direct.** We got tired of searching for the nearest town instead of a ski resort directly, White Weather supports direct ski resort searches and saves.
- **Detailed.** We know mountain weather changes fast. If the night forecast is vastly different than the day, we'll show you. Clear day and puking at night, no problem.
- **Seamless.** Widgets give you all the good stuff without a need to open the app. We know our way around iOS pretty well.
- **Fast.** We beleive weather data shouldn't take longer to load than it takes to walk outside. White Weather is optimized for speed, getting you out the door faster.

### Weather data

Provided by [NOAA](https://www.weather.gov) and [Open-Meteo](https://open-meteo.com).
- **NOAA forecast.weather.gov**, of course. We take as much as possible from the pros, including all written forecasts, 7-day symbols, and accumulation percent and amount.
- **Open-Meteo** we use as a background agent. You'll see this data in the hourly forecast, sunset/sunrise times, and wind data.
